Neely's Weekly Words

Arctic

Arctic

1. Areas in the North Pole


2. Very cold


Ex: The freezer is arctic.

Attitude

Attitude

1. A way of thinking or feeling about something


2. Sometimes meaning sassy or rude behavior


Ex: Billy had an attitude towards his mom.

Attract

Attract

1. Cause a specific reaction


2. Have a liking to something


Ex: Sally was attracted to Sammy.

Average

Average

1. Typical number or thing in data


2. Add all the things together, then divide by number of things, to get


Ex: The average number of bananas we have is six.

Avoid

Avoid

1. To stay away from something


2. To stop yourself from doing something


Ex: Timmy avoids nuts since he's allergic.

Ability

Ability

1. The physical or mental power or skill needed to do something


2. Talent, skill, or proficiency in a particular area


Ex: Ben has the ability to train animals.

Absorb

Absorb

1. To take in (knowledge, attitudes, etc.)


2. To engage or engross wholly


Ex: Paper absorbs water.

Access

Access

1. Permission, liberty, or ability to enter, approach, or pass to and from a place or to approach or communicate with a person or thing


2. A way or means of entering or approaching


Ex: The staircase gives access to the top floor.

Accuse

Accuse

1. To charge with a fault or offense


2. To charge with an offense judicially or by a public process


Ex: You can't accuse me of being selfish.

Act

Act

1. The doing of a thing


2. The process of doing something


Ex: Timmy acts weird today.

Ambition

Ambition

1. having an goal to be successful and achieve something


2. having a desire to complete a certain task


Ex: they were driven by their ambition to be a leader

Ancient

Ancient

1. Something from the past that is no longer in existence


2. A person who is old and has lived for a very long time


Ex: The pot is from an ancient civilization that no longer exists

Approach

Approach

1. To come closer to a person or an object


2. A new way of dealing with a task


Ex: We need a new approach to our project

Arrange

Arrange

1. To neatly organize things and put them in order


2. To make a plan for the future


Ex: The lady would like to arrange a meeting with the clerk

Active

Active

1. Ready to be or currently engaged in energetic events


2. A state of being present and focused


Ex: Ashley stays active by jogging every morning.

Actual

Actual

1. A state of being real or factual


2. Being real or existing


Ex: The actual size of my book is bigger than the pictures.

Adopt

Adopt

1. To voluntarily bring in a child or animal into your family


2. To take ownership or practice a new approach


Ex: The library adopted a new type of organization for their books.

Advantage

Advantage

1. A position to have a better chance at achieving something


2. More likely to succeed


Ex: George runs fast, so he has an advantage in the race.

Advice

Advice

1. Something you receive or give in an effort to make a situation better


2. Guidance for a future action


Ex: I could use some advice on how to study.
